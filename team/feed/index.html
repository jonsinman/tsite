President, TPA & MGA Operations, Mackinaw Administrators

Mr. Brown joined AmeriTrust as a result of the Mackinaw Administrators acquisition in 2015. He is responsible for managing and growing the national TPA as well as growing the MGA division, promoting all lines of coverage placed by AmeriTrust and its non-Affiliated carrier options.

Prior to this role, Mr. Brown was Senior Vice President of TPA and MGA operations. When Mr. Brown came on board, he had 25 years of insurance industry experience. As a Vice President with Aon, he was responsible for new business development including commercial P&C insurance brokerage, H&B brokerage, and business risk consulting including program analysis and eSolutions risk technologies.

Prior to Aon, he was the marketing manager for a medical cost containment company where he led a team that introduced medical bill review, pharmacy benefit management, automated provider payments, workflow eSolutions, medical case management, and vocational rehabilitation consulting to insurance carriers and self-insured employers on a national basis.

Mr. Brown began his claims career as an adjuster and eventually the claims manager of the Michigan Underground Storage Tank Financial Assurance program. This involved 7,200 environmental remediation claims across the state. In this role, he reported to the Michigan Department of Management and Budget and the Department of Environmental Quality. He assisted the MUSTFA fraud task force by working with the Michigan State Police and Attorney General’s office.

Mr. Brown has a Bachelor of Arts from Michigan State University, as well as the AIC designation from the Insurance Institute of America. He maintains a Resident Adjuster License and Resident Producer License for the State of Michigan.

Vice President, Human Resources

As Vice President, Human Resources, Mr. Wagner is a member of the Executive Management Team. Mr. Wagner is currently responsible for leading the Human Resources, Corporate Support Services, and Facility Management initiatives.

Mr. Wagner joined AmeriTrust Group, Inc. in June 1995 as a Financial Analyst handling cost accounting, budgeting and financial reporting duties for the fee-based operations. In 1999, he moved to Human Resources as the Benefits and Compensation Manager and was responsible for managing the company’s benefit and compensation programs. While in Human Resources, Mr. Wagner’s responsibilities have increased through the years to include managing of payroll, support services, facilities, human resources information systems, professional development, performance management, talent acquisition, and executive compensation. Mr. Wagner assumed his current role in 2014.

Prior to joining AmeriTrust Group, Inc., Mr. Wagner obtained a Bachelor’s Degree in Finance and Accounting from Central Michigan University and held various finance related positions in the insurance, banking, and defense industries. While at AmeriTrust Group, Inc., Mr. Wagner obtained a Master’s Degree in Human Resources Management from Central Michigan University, a Certified Compensation Professional (CCP) designation, a Senior Professional in Human Resources (SPHR) designation, and a Society of Human Resources Management Senior Certified Professional (SHRM-SCP) designation.

President of Admitted Carriers

John Baird is the President of AmeriTrust Group, Inc.’s Admitted Carriers: Star Insurance Company, Ameritrust Insurance Corporation, and Williamsburg National Insurance Company. He has twenty-eight years of property casualty experience, building core competencies in regional and corporate strategic planning, operational management, talent management, and financial management.

Prior to this appointment, he was the Regional Vice President of Liberty Mutual Group. In that role, Mr. Baird managed commercial underwriting and distribution operations for the states of Indiana and Michigan, along with serving as a member of the executive team, with oversight of the Midwest Region, encompassing a nine-state geography. He also served on the Board of Directors for Liberty Mutual Group companies domiciled in the state of Indiana, he was an Executive Committee member serving on the Insurance Institute of Indiana’s Board of Directors, and a board member for the Indiana Insurance Guaranty Association.

Mr. Baird obtained a Bachelor of Science in Business Insurance from Ferris State University in 1989. He began his insurance career as a Commercial Rater with Ohio Casualty in Royal Oak, Michigan. Mr. Baird completed Ohio Casualty’s Underwriter Training Program in 1991, after which he held commercial field positions in underwriting and marketing. He became an Ohio Casualty Executive Officer for Personal Lines in 2001, supporting the reorganization of Personal Lines operations. In this role, he was responsible for underwriting and distribution operations for the western half of the United States. In 2005, he became the Marketing Vice President for Personal Lines. In 2007, Liberty Mutual purchased Ohio Casualty, and Mr. Baird became Regional Vice President for Commercial Lines, overseeing underwriting and distribution operations for Ohio and Kentucky. In 2012, Mr. Baird became Regional Vice President for the Midwest region of Business Insurance.

President of Century Insurance Group

Mr. Cheung Kwan is the President of the Century Insurance Group, AmeriTrust’s Excess and Surplus Lines companies.  He is responsible for developing the business strategies and overseeing the day-to-day operations to enable Century to continue its profitable growth trajectory.

Mr. Kwan has 30 years of extensive experience with all aspects of insurance company operations. His background includes extensive reserving and pricing (including predictive modeling) expertise. In addition, he has had extensive experience working closely with Claims, IT, Underwriting, Finance, Operations, and Reinsurance, including the structuring of captives. He has excellent communication skills and has a proven track record of leadership, talent development, and strategic thinking.

He most recently served as the Vice President and Senior Pricing Actuary for Hallmark Financial Services. He began his career at Geico in 1986, and spent ten years with Liberty Mutual Insurance providing Actuarial leadership on reserving and pricing. He worked within the Claims Department at the Hartford for five years, providing support for complex asbestos and environmental claims. He was with Zurich Insurance for seven years, as a senior leader within transportation programs, specialties, and group captives and finally as the Global Chief Pricing Actuary for Property Lines.

Mr. Kwan is a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania with degrees in Math and Psychology, and has been a Fellow of the Casualty Actuarial Society (FCAS) since 1996.

Chief Underwriting Officer

Fred Browning is Chief Underwriting Officer for AmeriTrust Group, Inc. He is responsible for the overall underwriting portfolio strategy, underwriting appetite, monitoring industry trends, product management, and effective controls for all lines of business. He and his staff of Underwriting Leaders also provide referral underwriting support to the Admitted and Non-Admitted underwriters.

Prior to assuming the role of Chief Underwriting Officer, Mr. Browning was Senior Vice President of Admitted Carriers for AmeriTrust Group, Inc.

Mr. Browning has nearly 30 years of insurance industry experience. Most recently, he spent the past 4 years as Managing Partner for Overland Group, LLC, where his responsibilities included strategic planning, product research and development, reinsurance placement, and program management. From 2002 to 2012, Mr. Browning held executive positions at 3 national insurance carriers with profit and loss responsibilities, strategic planning for profit and growth, product and market segment development, and distribution management.

From 1987 to 2002, Mr. Browning held a variety of executive positions including Chairman, President and CEO of Chrysler Insurance Group, where he had full operational responsibilities for sales, marketing, underwriting, finance, legal, claims, mergers & acquisitions, and reinsurance.

Mr. Browning received his Bachelor’s Degree from Georgetown College and completed the Executive Business Program at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. He holds Charter Property Casualty Underwriter (CPCU) and Associate in Risk Management (ARM) designations.

Chief Claims Officer

Ms. McGuire joined the company in 2012 as a Claims Attorney managing a book of professional liability claims. Thereafter, she served in various roles of claims management, including oversight of the Specialty, Complex and Coverage groups, as well as the Vice President of Casualty Litigation.

Prior to joining AmeriTrust, Ms. McGuire was a litigation partner at an Ohio law firm for 13 years and specialized in business litigation and medical malpractice defense. She received her Bachelor’s and Master’s Degrees in Speech Communications from Miami University in Oxford, Ohio. Thereafter, she received her Juris Doctorate from The Ohio State University College of Law. She was a Managing Editor for the OHIO STATE LAW JOURNAL and was awarded the Alumni Special Recognition Award for outstanding achievement in scholarship and leadership. In 2005, she was recognized as a recipient of Business First’s Forty Under 40 Award in Columbus, Ohio. She was recognized as an Ohio Rising Star in 2009, 2011 and 2012. She is Certified in Mediation by the Institute for Conflict Management. Ms. McGuire has also served as an Adjunct Instructor in the Department of Communication at Otterbein University and taught Business, Interpersonal and Organizational Communication courses.

Ms. McGuire has served as an author, speaker or panelist for industry events. Her activity includes Don’t Be The One To Pay The Price: Avoiding Bad Faith (panelist, Eagle International Associates, 2019); Selecting The Right Expert For Your Case (panelist, Magna Legal Services, 2018); Liability Limitations in Contract (presentation, DRI Construction Law Seminar, 2018); Risky Business – Dealing with Difficult Professionals As Insureds/Clients (panelist, CLM 2017); Defense Counsel Challenges in Complying With Carrier Guidelines (presentation, CLM 2015); How To Negotiate and Drive Early, Favorable Settlements (presentation, 2015); Women Lawyers: Defeating Public Speaking Fears (Article, Woman Advocate, ABA Journal, 2009).

Ms. McGuire is actively involved with the Homeless Families Foundation for Franklin County, Ohio and the Ohio South Chapter of the Hugh O’Brian Youth Foundation. She is a member of the Defense Research Institute and the Claims and Litigation Management Alliance.
